What counts as “Radio Support”?

Installation, programming, coverage planning, interference checks, antenna tuning, licensing assistance, firmware updates, repairs, and user training—delivered on-site or remote.

Two‑Way & LMR (Commercial)

Support for handhelds, mobiles, and repeaters used by logistics, hospitality, security, and construction—often on UHF/VHF with analog, DMR, NXDN, or P25 systems.

Amateur / Ham Radio

Station build‑outs, antenna selection, feedline testing, grounding, digital modes (FT8, D‑STAR), and contest‑ready performance for licensed operators.

Broadcast & Events

RF planning for OB vans and stages, intercoms (UHF/DECT), IFB, STL links, temporary spectrum coordination, and failover plans for live shows.

Step‑by‑Step: Getting the Right Support

  1. Define the use‑case: coverage area, user count, indoors/outdoors, and any high‑noise zones.
  2. Inventory your gear: radio models, firmware, batteries, headsets, antennas, repeaters, cables.
  3. Check compliance: licenses or permit requirements for your jurisdiction and band plan.
  4. Ask providers for a site survey: they should measure RSSI/SINR and assess line‑of‑sight and intermod risks.
  5. Review a written support plan: SLAs, response times, loaner units, spares, and maintenance cycles.
  6. Run a pilot: test channels/codeplugs, record call quality and dead zones before full rollout.
  7. Document & train: quick‑start guides, channel maps, and a contact tree for incidents.

What a Good Provider Delivers

  • Coverage heatmaps and interference analysis.
  • Codeplug programming and fleet templates.
  • On‑site installation, testing & training.
  • SLA‑backed support with spares and loaners.

Questions to Ask

  • What radios & bands do you specialize in (UHF/VHF/700‑800MHz)?
  • Do you provide licensing guidance and frequency coordination?
  • What’s your average response time and escalation path?
  • Can you share references from similar deployments?
Pro tip: Ask for a codeplug backup and a simple change‑request process so channel updates don’t break your fleet.
